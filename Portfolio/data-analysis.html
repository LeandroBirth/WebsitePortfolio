R Language Data Analysis: Statistical Analysis of Aedes aegypti Sterilization Rate by Gamma Radiation

Case - Radiation Sterilization Experiment

I conducted a Statistical Analysis to assess the effects of radiation doses on the proportion of Aedes aegypti mosquito larval births. This research involved analyzing binary data using R, focusing on two radiation doses (25 Gy and 30 Gy) across four experimental groups.

Objective

  • Assess the impact of different radiation doses on mosquito sterilization effectiveness.
  • Model the proportion of larval births to understand variation between groups and treatments.
  • Ensure proper model fit by addressing issues like overdispersion or underdispersion.

Approach

  • Used a Binomial Regression Model to analyze binary response data (success or failure of larval births).
  • Applied Generalized Linear Models (GLMs) with a logistic link function to model the expected birth proportions.
  • Adjusted for overdispersion and underdispersion by estimating a dispersion parameter using quasi-maximum likelihood methods.
  • Validated model fit using deviance, degrees of freedom, and residual analysis (envelope plots).

Key Results

  • The 30 Gy radiation dose significantly reduced larval births compared to the 25 Gy dose.
  • Subdispersion was detected, and a dispersion parameter of 0.0404 was applied to improve model fit.
  • The final model showed adequate fit, with a deviance of 12.46 and 13 degrees of freedom.

Tools & Technologies

  • R Language for statistical computing and data visualization.
  • Functions and packages for GLMs and dispersion parameter estimation.
  • Custom R scripts for data preprocessing, model fitting, and residual diagnostics.
